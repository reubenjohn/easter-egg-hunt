**Task eggs do NOT contain Skittles** — their bonus is awarded after completing the challenge.

### Fairness Note

Each player gets the same Skittle distribution (9/6/3 split) so no one has an inherent advantage. Shuffle the eggs after stuffing so YOU don't know which eggs have more Skittles when hiding.

## 4. Assembly Instructions

### What You Need at the Prep Table

- [ ] All 200 plastic egg shells (10 solid colors + two-tone pink/purple)
- [ ] ~300 individual Skittles (1 full 36oz bag is ~800+ Skittles, so a 14oz bag is plenty with buffer)
- [ ] 17 printed task card slips (from `tasks-print.html`)
- [ ] 5 printed power-up card slips (from `tasks-print.html`)
- [ ] Scissors (for cutting printed cards)
- [ ] 9 gallon-size Ziploc bags (to sort player eggs after stuffing)
- [ ] 1 gallon-size Ziploc bag (for special eggs)
- [ ] Marker/pen (to label bags)
- [ ] Small bowl (for Skittles staging)

### Step-by-Step

#### Step A: Print and Cut Cards (~10 min)

1. Open `tasks-print.html` in a browser (locally or at https://reubenjohn.github.io/easter-egg-hunt/tasks-print.html)
2. Click **Print Cards** button
3. Print on regular paper (color preferred for visual appeal, B&W works too)
4. Cut out all 22 cards (17 task + 5 power-up) along the card borders
5. Fold each card slip so it fits inside a plastic egg

#### Step B: Sort Egg Shells by Color (~5 min)

1. Open all egg packages
2. Separate into 10 piles by color (9 player colors + special pink/purple shells)
3. Count to verify: 18 per player color, at least 22 special shells
4. Set aside 16 spare special shells in a bag

#### Step C: Stuff Player Eggs (~45-60 min)

Work one color at a time. For each player's 18 eggs:

1. **Lay out 18 empty egg halves** (bottom halves open, top halves ready)
2. **Count out 30 Skittles** into a bowl
3. **Distribute Skittles:**
   - Drop **1 Skittle** into each of the first 9 eggs
   - Drop **2 Skittles** into each of the next 6 eggs
   - Drop **3 Skittles** into each of the last 3 eggs
4. **Snap all 18 eggs closed** — press firmly so they don't pop open
5. **Shuffle the 18 eggs** so you can't tell which has more (mix them around on the table)
6. **Place all 18 into a labeled Ziploc bag** (write player name on bag)
7. **Repeat for all 9 players**

**Pro tip:** Do this assembly-line style — Reuben does one color while Raina does another. Cuts time in half.

#### Step D: Stuff Task Eggs (~15 min)

1. Take 17 special egg shells matching the difficulty colors:
   - 5 all-pink shells (easy)
   - 5 pink-top/white-bottom shells (medium)
   - 4 pink-top/purple-bottom shells (hard)
   - 3 all-purple shells (extreme)
2. Place the matching task card slip inside each egg
3. **Do NOT add Skittles** — task egg rewards are given after task completion
4. Snap closed
5. Place all 17 task eggs into the special eggs Ziploc bag

#### Step E: Stuff Power-Up Eggs (~5 min)

1. Take 5 special egg shells (purple top / player-color bottom):
   - Choose 5 different player colors for the bottom halves
2. Place one power-up card slip inside each egg
3. **Do NOT add Skittles**
4. Snap closed
5. Add to the special eggs Ziploc bag (with task eggs)

#### Step F: Final Verification (~5 min)

- [ ] Count all bags: 9 player bags + 1 special bag = 10 bags
- [ ] Each player bag has exactly 18 eggs
- [ ] Special bag has exactly 22 eggs (17 task + 5 power-up)
- [ ] 16 spare shells set aside
- [ ] All eggs snap closed firmly (do a shake test — nothing should rattle open)
- [ ] Label the special eggs bag clearly: "SPECIAL EGGS - DO NOT OPEN"

---

## 5. Storage & Transport

- Store stuffed egg bags in a **large tote bag or box** — keep upright to prevent eggs from popping open
- Transport in a car — avoid stacking heavy items on top of the egg box
- Bring egg bags **inside a cooler or insulated bag** if it's warm out (Skittles can get sticky in heat)
- Keep the special eggs bag **separate and accessible** — you'll distribute these differently during hiding
